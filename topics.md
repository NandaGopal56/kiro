## Topics to implement - 


1. **Guardrails & Safety Controls** – Designing robust safeguards for AI systems, including input/output validation, tool-call verification, access control, rate limiting, and policy enforcement to ensure safe and reliable operation.

2. **Agent & RAG Evaluation** – Measuring the quality and reliability of agents and retrieval systems using metrics such as faithfulness, context recall, answer relevance, BLEU, ROUGE, perplexity, and task success rates.

3. **Observability & Monitoring** – Building visibility into agent pipelines through tracing, logging, metrics, alerts, and continuous performance monitoring to diagnose issues and optimize behavior.

4. **Prompt Caching & Reuse** – Reducing latency and cost by intelligently caching prompts, intermediate results, and model responses across repeated workflows.

5. **Context Management & Pruning** – Managing growing conversation history through summarization, compression, prioritization, and selective context retention to stay within model limits.

6. **Cost Optimization Strategies** – Controlling LLM infrastructure costs through model routing, caching, batching, token optimization, and efficient workflow design.

7. **Model Fine-Tuning & Adaptation** – Customizing foundation models using supervised fine-tuning, preference optimization, and domain-specific training techniques.

8. **Advanced RAG Architectures** – Building high-quality retrieval systems with optimized chunking, retrieval strategies, reranking, multimodal retrieval, and knowledge-grounded generation.

9. **Graph RAG & Knowledge Graphs** – Enhancing retrieval and reasoning by leveraging graph structures, entity relationships, and knowledge graphs alongside traditional vector search.

10. **Vector Databases & Embeddings** – Storing, indexing, and querying embeddings efficiently using vector databases to power semantic search and memory systems.

11. **Modern Retrieval Techniques** – Implementing vector search, BM25, hybrid search, MMR, Reciprocal Rank Fusion (RRF), and other ranking strategies for better information retrieval.

12. **Long-Running Agent Workflows** – Designing agents that can execute complex tasks over extended periods while maintaining state, progress, and reliability.

13. **Human-in-the-Loop Systems** – Integrating human review, approvals, feedback, and interruption handling into autonomous agent workflows.

14. **Dynamic Model Steering** – Guiding model behavior during generation through contextual signals, constraints, retrieval, memory, and runtime instructions.

15. **Tool Calling & MCP Integration** – Enabling agents to interact with external systems, APIs, and tools through structured function calling and the Model Context Protocol (MCP).

16. **Goal Planning & Task Tracking** – Equipping agents with goals, task decomposition, TODO lists, progress tracking, and completion verification mechanisms.

17. **Memory Architectures for Agents** – Managing episodic, semantic, and procedural memory to support personalization, learning, and long-term task execution.

18. **Agent Learning & Self-Reflection** – Implementing background learning, self-evaluation, reflection loops, planning, and "dreaming" mechanisms to improve future performance.

19. **Multi-Agent Collaboration** – Building systems where specialized agents coordinate, communicate, and hand off tasks to solve complex problems collaboratively.

20. **Vectorless RAG & Document Navigation** – Retrieving information through structured indexes, page references, metadata, and hierarchical navigation without relying solely on embeddings.

21. **Prompt Versioning & Experimentation** – Managing prompt evolution through version control, benchmarking, A/B testing, and performance comparisons.

22. **Agent Testing & Harnesses** – Creating testing frameworks and benchmark environments to systematically validate agent behavior, reliability, and regressions.

23. **Workflow Recovery & Partial Retries** – Recovering from failures by resuming workflows from intermediate states rather than restarting entire executions.

24. **Agent Loops & Iterative Reasoning** – Designing feedback-driven execution loops where agents continuously plan, act, evaluate, and refine their approach.

25. **Checkpointing, Retries & Error Recovery** – Implementing checkpoints, targeted retries, and corrective steering strategies to improve robustness in long-running agent systems.
